Sum of 3 Squares Theorem¶

$ D = \square + \square + \square$ iff $ D \equiv 0,4,7 \mod 8$

First paragraph of arXiv:1502.04209 Integer points and their orthogonal lattices. For a proof they refer to Disquisitiones.

Example There exist a number $t < p$ such that $a^t \equiv 1 \mod p$.

Proof The sequence $1, a, a^2 , \dots, a^{p-1}$ runs through some the residues $\{1,2,\dots, p-1\}$. By pigeonhole, there must be $a^m \equiv a^n \mod p$, and therefore $a^{m-n} \equiv 1 \mod p$.

This is the first result in Chapter 3. This doesn't quite tell us that $m-n \big| p-1$.
